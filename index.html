The process

Here's exactly what I do for you.

No vague promises. This is the real, step-by-step process I follow on every single case — total loss or theft.

  1. 01

    You send me the insurer's valuation report.

    Share the offer your insurance company gave you, along with the year, make, model, trim, options and kilometres of your vehicle. That's all I need to get started.

  2. 02

    I compare it to live market data.

    I use a real-time market data tool that shows current retail prices for comparable vehicles — same year, make, model, options and mileage. Sometimes the initial offer is actually fair. If it is, I'll tell you honestly and you can move forward with confidence. If it isn't, we move to the next step.

  3. 03

    You formally put the insurer on notice.

    You send a simple email to your insurer stating that Write Off Whiz will be representing you in all matters going forward. From that point, I take over all communication. I copy you on every email and update you on phone conversations.

  4. 04

    I tear apart their comparables — and build better ones.

    The vehicles insurance companies use for comparison are often "as is" sales, have accident history, out-of-province history, were former daily rentals or fleet vehicles, are missing options, or are private-sale listings that may be fake or from unlicensed "curbsiders." Many listed prices are finance-buyer pricing only — cash price can be thousands more. I identify every flaw in their list, then build my own list of properly priced, fully equipped, safety-certified comparables that give an accurate picture of what your vehicle is worth.

  5. 05

    I negotiate directly with the adjuster — and their management.

    Armed with the evidence, I negotiate on your behalf until we reach a fair and equitable settlement. You only pay me if I win, and only after you've received payment from your insurer. You are never out of pocket.

Before you do anything

The insurance company is not on your side.
Here's what not to do.

The adjuster's job is to close your file quickly and cheaply. These are the mistakes that cost people thousands of dollars.

  • ✕

    Don't accept the first offer.

    The initial valuation is almost never their best offer. It's a starting point — and they're counting on you not knowing that.

  • ✕

    Don't let them pressure you.

    Tactics like threatening to remove your rental car are designed to make you rush. Take your time. Their pressure is a negotiation strategy, not a deadline.

  • ✕

    Don't assume. Read your policy.

    Know your coverages for rental cars, repair-facility storage, and replacement transport. Don't trust the adjuster to know the fine print of your own policy — that's their job, or mine.

  • ✕

    Don't let emotions take over.

    The adjuster doesn't care about the memories you have in that vehicle. To them it's a file number. Keeping emotions out of negotiations is something I do for you.

  • ✕

    Don't fight a battle you're not equipped to win.

    Insurance companies do this every single day. If you go in without industry knowledge and live market data, you will lose.

  • ✕

    Don't guess at your car's value.

    Sentiment and guesswork won't hold up. The only thing that wins is real, current, comparable market data — and I have the tools to pull it.
